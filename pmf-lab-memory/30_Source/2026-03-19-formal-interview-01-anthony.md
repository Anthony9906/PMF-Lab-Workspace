## Key evidence captured

- Anthony works inside a first-tier non-standard equipment manufacturer serving 3C consumer electronics, new-energy vehicle, and OEM customers.
- The product innovation team sits inside the company and reports directly to the chairman.
- The team can potentially access both real users and decision-makers across functions when the scenario has enough strategic value.
- Internal data access is strong for engineering drawings, RFQ packages, historical quotations, NCR or change records, BOMs, standard-part libraries, and procurement records.
- Most of the relevant information has already been digitized in systems such as PLM and ERP.
- The company has already started validating OPP-011 on cylinder selection and has early positive progress.
- In OPP-011, selection mistakes can cause engineering changes, longer delivery, extra labor input, dead inventory, poor standardization, and higher procurement difficulty.
- Anthony judges standard-part selection and quotation as the closest-to-money scenarios.
- For OPP-001, the accessible quoting domains include machining parts, sheet metal, and customer-facing non-standard equipment quotations.
- The main quoting difficulties are drawing understanding, process judgment, and cost-breakdown quality.
- A quotation-management system was already built internally, but only part of the equipment BOM and pricing data has been loaded so far.
- For OPP-002, the real scenario is customer-site FACA reporting during installation, debugging, trial production, and mass-production stages for 3C projects.
- In that OPP-002 scenario, engineers must collect NG images and system logs, coordinate root-cause analysis, and submit reports quickly to avoid customer complaints.
- The customer-site environment has strict security checks, and devices entering the site cannot connect to the internet.
- Internal engineering drawings and documents are easier to access than supplier-side revision workflows.
- Anthony's current preference is: primary line OPP-011, backup line OPP-001.

## Interview implications

- OPP-011 now has the strongest combination of strategic fit, data access, and active validation momentum.
- OPP-001 is supported by strong internal data access and a partially built quotation system, making it a realistic backup line.
- OPP-002 has a concrete and painful workflow, but deployment constraints are materially harder because of offline and high-security customer sites.
- OPP-003 should replace OPP-009 in the formal top-6 working set because internal engineering-document access is easier than supplier-side access.
- Future prioritization should weight access reality more heavily, not only first-principles scoring.
