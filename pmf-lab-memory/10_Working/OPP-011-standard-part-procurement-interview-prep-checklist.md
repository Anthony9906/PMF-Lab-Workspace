# OPP-011 Standard-Part Procurement Interview Prep Checklist

## Goal

Prepare only the material needed to make the procurement interview concrete and decision-useful.

Do not delay the interview by waiting for perfect analysis.

## Priority Rule

### Must have before interview

- one-page description of the current cylinder agent use case
- 2 to 3 concrete cylinder cases to use as examples
- a rough map of who currently touches the standard-part procurement workflow
- basic supplier and purchasing context that prevents naive questions

### Strongly recommended before interview

- recent purchase statistics for cylinders over the last 3 to 6 months
- top purchased models or SKUs
- rough supplier distribution for cylinder purchases
- any known preferred-part or preferred-supplier logic

### Nice to have but not blocking

- first-pass profile of possible cost-down opportunities
- rough estimate of tail SKUs, fragmented models, or repeated substitutions
- category-level comparison of prices across equivalent options

## Recommended Prep Work

### 1. Understand supplier basics

Yes, do this before the interview.
You do not need a perfect supplier map, but you should know:

- main cylinder suppliers or brands
- whether preferred suppliers exist
- whether there are backup suppliers
- whether some models are single-source in practice

This prevents shallow interview questions and helps you interpret supplier-option logic correctly.

### 2. Pull 3 to 6 months of purchase statistics

Yes, this is one of the highest-value prep items.

Recommended window:

- default: 6 months if the data is easy to pull
- fallback: 3 months if data extraction is hard or recent demand is more representative

Minimum fields to request:

- purchase order date
- supplier
- model or SKU
- quantity
- unit price
- total amount
- requester or demand source if available

Minimum outputs to prepare:

- total purchase line count
- total quantity
- top 20 models by purchase frequency
- top 20 models by total quantity
- top suppliers by line count or quantity

### 3. Build a first-pass cylinder purchasing profile

Yes, but keep it light.
The goal is not to finish the analysis before the interview.
The goal is to enter the interview with a few grounded hypotheses such as:

- a small number of models account for most purchases
- too many low-frequency SKUs may exist
- supplier choices may be narrower than engineering assumes
- some equivalent models may have meaningful price gaps

### 4. Draft possible cost-down hypotheses

Yes, but only as hypotheses to test, not conclusions.

Useful hypothesis types:

- preferred models have better negotiated prices
- some engineering-selected models unnecessarily reduce supplier optionality
- low-frequency SKUs may create avoidable cost or inventory complexity
- equivalent options may exist with lower price or better supply flexibility

### 5. Other recommended prep work

- identify whether there is a formal preferred-part list, preferred-supplier list, or exception approval rule
- clarify which system holds the most reliable price record
- clarify whether procurement values unit price, lead time, supply stability, or standardization most
- prepare 2 to 3 real examples where engineering selection and procurement logic may differ
- prepare one simple slide or page showing what the agent currently does in cylinder selection

## What Should Not Block The Interview

- perfect spend analysis
- full cost-saving model
- complete supplier master data cleanup
- exact equivalent-part mapping for all cylinders

## Best Interview Input Pack

Go into the interview with this minimum pack:

- short description of the current agent capability
- 2 to 3 real cylinder cases
- one table of recent purchasing statistics
- one list of top models and top suppliers
- one page of open questions you want procurement to settle
